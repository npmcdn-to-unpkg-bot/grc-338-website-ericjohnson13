A Little About Me...

My name is Eric Johnson. I'm originally from Fullerton, Orange County, CA. I think the easiest way to describe me is to say that I collect as many talents as possible and become mediocre at all of them. It's interesting to see how these talents have developed over time.

When I was in 5th grade, I started playing the saxophone. I wanted to be the next Paul Desmond or Charlie Parker (minus the heavy smoking or heroin addiction). I actually turned out to be a pretty good saxophone player and was playing in jazz bands by the age of 14. I probably would have continued on that path had it not been for another creative path that grabbed my attention.

During 6th grade I had this intense desire to be the lead in the the 6th grade play. This was a big deal to me for some reason. It was strange, especially for a kid who had anxiety presenting in front of large groups of people. Why would I want to put myself in a position to not only present in front of hundreds of people, but sing and dance while doing it.

It turns out that after mustering up enough courage to audition (and after the luck of getting cast as the lead), I found a new love in the theatre. I spent the rest of my school years performing in school musicals and designing lighting for productions as well.

I attended my first year of university at Cal Poly State University San Luis Obispo as a Computer Science major. I then took 2 years off from school to serve as a missionary for my church in Costa Rica. While in Costa Rica I determined that I wanted to devote my studies to music. When I returned to the states, I transfered to Brigham Young University to study music.

I spend two years at BYU working to be a music major, but after failing my audition, I fell back to my love of technology and finish my undergrade in Eletrical Engineering. Upon graduation I returned to the central coast of California, working for Cal Poly in the Graphic Communication department.
